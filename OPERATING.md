# echoes operating rules

This repo is a proof trail for learning in public.

The writing should stay handwritten: rough, specific, and honest.

## Core Rule

Do not publish or commit a learning claim unless I can explain it without notes.

## What Counts As A Daily Echo

At least one of these:

- a concept explained in my own words
- a script I typed or modified
- a metric or result I measured
- a failure I understood better
- a question I can now answer
- a tweet draft based on real work

## AI Boundary

AI may help with:

- structure
- review
- finding gaps
- asking self-test questions
- making wording clearer after I write the raw version

AI should not:

- invent what I learned
- make the note sound more advanced than my understanding
- fill quiet days with fake progress
- turn every tweet into generic inspiration
- remove the useful rough edges from the thought

## Daily Voice

Good daily note:

```text
Today I learned why 16 kHz audio has 16,000 samples per second.
I wrote a sine wave script and realized frequency changes how quickly the wave oscillates.
Still fuzzy: why the Fourier transform finds frequencies.
```

Bad daily note:

```text
Explored cutting-edge voice AI systems and gained deep insight into speech processing.
```

The first one is real. The second one is fog.

## Tweet Style

Tweet one concrete learning edge:

- what clicked
- what broke
- what surprised me
- what I misunderstood
- what I built
- what I can now explain simply

Avoid:

- fake certainty
- startup-speak
- "day N of becoming cracked"
- over-polished threads
- generic lessons anyone could say

## Commit Rule

Commit the trace even when it is small.

Suggested commit messages:

```text
Add note on audio sampling
Add tweet draft for STFT lesson
Log VAD failure notes
Add weekly voice AI recap
```

## Weekly Rule

Every week should have:

- at least 3 daily notes
- at least 3 tweet drafts
- at least 1 weekly recap
- at least 1 concrete code/proof artifact linked from the notes
